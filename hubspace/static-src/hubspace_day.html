About me

I am a collaborative systems architect and Internet technologist. I am involved with developing Free and Open Source software with the goal of increasing collaboration and knowledge sharing between social enterprises.

One of my most recent projects is the software that this site is running. I am currently working for the Hub improving this system and making newer better ones. Maybe one day we will be better than facebook! But seriously the plan now is to build virtual organisation tools to create an global social enterprise platform rooted in the physical communities provided by Hubs. Coincidentally, I did a Masters thesis on "Virtual Open Organisational Platforms".

I have studied both Economics and Computing at University and have a keen interest in subverting conventional wisdom and practice in both areas. I don't know maybe I am just subversive by nature? I also co-founded http://uniteddiversity.com and http://open.coop and am still working with the people involved in those projects.

The reason I got involved in software and technology (some years ago) was due to a realisation that the world needs new monetary systems and the internet is the best hope of a platform for free and democratic money. I recently saw this 50 minute film which summarized very well a few years of my thinking on what is wrong with the world's monetary system. http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-9050474362583451279 Of course it doesn't cover in any detail the possible solutions but you can't learn everything in 50mins.

N.B. I get really annoyed when people who think they are socially progressive or ethical, start talking about intellectual property and copyright as if they are there to help small business and inventors. I can't understand why it is not obvious that a society in which all information is privately owned can be free or democratic. One of my favourite presentations is "free culture" by Laurence Lessig since it makes this really clear. I also love the style of the presentation http://randomfoo.net/oscon/2002/lessig/free.html
